The building depicted on your flyer — an 8-story boutique hotel with ground-floor restaurant — is a **60-80 key hotel**. Industry math on that:

- Minimum land footprint for a project at that scale: **~20,000-25,000 sq ft** (0.46-0.57 acres).

**The rendering depicts a project that needs roughly 4-5x the land you currently control.**

This is not a criticism — it's a diagnosis. The hotel you drew is the right hotel for the neighborhood. It's just not buildable on the three parcels you own today.

## 6. What "Pre-Development Funding Mechanism" Actually Means

Your flyer asks the developer to *"have your own funding mechanism to finance predevelopment."* Let's make that concrete.

**Pre-development cost (before a shovel hits dirt):**

| Activity | Cost | Timeline |
|----------|------|----------|
| Land options & earnest deposits | $100K - $150K | Month 1-3 |
| Environmental Phase I + II | $30K - $75K | Month 2-4 |
| Concept architecture + site planning | $100K - $200K | Month 3-8 |
| Zoning / CUP / rezoning legal & filing | $50K - $100K | Month 4-12 |
| Civil engineering / survey | $40K - $75K | Month 4-9 |
| Market study + hotel feasibility | $40K - $60K | Month 2-5 |
| Capital stack structuring (NMTC legal, OZ legal, CRA application) | $75K - $150K | Month 6-15 |
| Project management / carrying costs | $150K - $300K | Ongoing |
| **Total Pre-Development** | **$600K - $1.1M** | **15-18 months** |

This is money spent **before any revenue, before any construction loan funds, and before you know for certain the project will close.** A meaningful portion of this money is at risk — if entitlements fail or capital doesn't stack, pre-development dollars don't come back.

**This is the "funding mechanism" question.** A developer who brings their own pre-development capital is accepting six-figure personal risk for 15+ months to see if the deal will happen. That's a very different contribution than land ownership.

### Three Fair Structures to Consider

**Option 1: Land Seller + Restaurant Operator**
- You sell your 3 parcels to the development entity at fair market value (we'd target **$600K-$800K cash**, paid at closing — roughly 20-25x assessed value, very favorable).
- You sign a **long-term below-market lease for Ollie's Trolley** in the ground-floor restaurant space (50% of market rent for 20 years, renewal options).
- You get **naming rights** on the restaurant; potentially co-branding on the hotel.
- **You own: 0% of the hotel, 100% of Ollie's Trolley operation.**
- **Upside:** cash now + your restaurant in a Class A building with no rent burden.
- **Downside:** no equity in the hotel itself.

**Option 2: Land Contribution as Equity (Our Recommendation)**
- You contribute your 3 parcels to the development entity at **appraised value** (~$700K).
- That land contribution becomes a **pari passu equity position** in the deal.
- Your implied equity share: **~10-12% of GP + LP equity combined** (reflecting that land is ~$700K of the ~$6M total equity raise).
- You also get the below-market restaurant lease and naming rights from Option 1.
- **You own: ~10-12% of the hotel, 100% of Ollie's Trolley.**
- **Upside:** real equity in a $22-27M asset. Your 10% could be worth $2-3M at stabilization/sale. Plus restaurant operation.
- **Downside:** illiquid for 7-10 years; you're a passive minority owner on the hotel side.

**Option 3: Land Contribution + Modified Preferred Equity**
- Same as Option 2, but your land contribution is structured as **preferred equity** instead of common.
- You receive an 8% annual preferred return on your $700K contribution (~$56K/year), paid when cash flow allows.
- You also retain a small **residual equity participation** (3-5%) after the preferred return is satisfied.
- **Upside:** some yield during operation + small back-end upside + restaurant operation.
- **Downside:** smaller back-end than Option 2 if the project performs well.

### What 49% Would Actually Require From You

To fairly own **49%** of a $28M-$30M project, you'd need to contribute approximately:

- Your land (~$700K), **plus**
- Additional equity capital of **~$6-7M**, **plus**
- Co-sign the construction loan personal guarantee (~$17M contingent liability), **plus**
- Take on ~50% of pre-development risk ($450K-$550K of at-risk capital).

That's the math. 49% ownership corresponds to 49% of the **capital and risk**, not 49% of the land. This is how institutional capital partners evaluate deals — and it's why the flyer's structure won't attract serious developers.

But we can get you to a position that **feels like ownership, pays like ownership, and protects the Ollie's Trolley legacy** — without requiring you to write a $7M check.

## Appendix B — Glossary

- **CRA (Community Reinvestment Area):** Ohio property tax abatement program. Cincinnati's "Lift" tier = 100% abatement for 15 years on qualifying new construction.
- **NMTC (New Markets Tax Credit):** Federal tax credit that subsidizes development in qualified low-income census tracts. Flows through Community Development Entities (CDEs).
- **OZ (Opportunity Zone):** Federal program offering capital gains tax benefits for investment in designated tracts. **This site is not in an OZ.**
- **TIF (Tax Increment Financing):** Local mechanism that captures future property tax growth to fund infrastructure. Not currently overlaying this site.
- **GP / LP:** General Partner (the developer, active operator) / Limited Partner (passive equity investor).
- **Yield on Cost:** Annual NOI ÷ total development cost. Below cap rate = project worth less than it cost to build.
- **Type III vs Type I construction:** Wood over concrete podium (Type III, up to 5-6 stories, cheaper) vs steel/concrete highrise (Type I, taller, much more expensive).
