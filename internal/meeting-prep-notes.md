# Friday Meeting — Internal Prep Notes

## Meeting Objective

Have an honest, non-confrontational conversation with Marvin about the **deal mechanics** — not the equity ask. Show him what the project actually needs to be real, what parts he can realistically own, and position Co-Llab as his pre-development advisor.

## What NOT to Say

- ❌ "Your 49% ask is unrealistic."
- ❌ "You don't understand development."
- ❌ "No developer will ever take this deal."
- ❌ "Your land is too small."

## What TO Say Instead

- ✅ "Your flyer shows a real vision — an 8-story boutique hotel. Let's talk about what it takes to build that."
- ✅ "The math on the rendering versus the math on your 6,000 sq ft tells a specific story."
- ✅ "The corner at 1601 Central is a genuinely valuable position. Here's what it enables."
- ✅ "The CRA 'Lift' tier is a meaningful subsidy — this site has more going for it than most West End lots."
- ✅ "The 49% number isn't wrong or right on its own — it depends on what you're contributing to. Let's walk through both sides."

## Conversation Flow (60-90 min)

### Opening (5 min)
Let Marvin talk. Ask him to describe his vision in his own words.
- Why a hotel? Why now? Why here?
- What does success look like for him?
- What's his connection to Ollie's Trolley — is he the owner, franchisee, family?
- Has he talked to other developers? What have they said?

**Listen for:** whether his real priority is cash, control, legacy, the restaurant, or something else. This determines which equity structure we recommend.

### The Site (10 min)
Walk through Section 2 of the briefing.
- Here's what you own, exactly.
- Here's the zoning.
- Here's the CRA tier and why it matters.
- Here's where you are on the block.

**Key point:** You have a real asset. Not nothing. But specific.

### The Rendering vs. The Land (10 min)
Walk through Section 3.
- The building in your flyer is a 60-80 key hotel.
- That needs ~25,000-30,000 sq ft of land.
- You have 6,000 sq ft.
- This doesn't mean the project is impossible. It means the project in the picture and the project on your parcels are two different projects.

**Goal:** Get him to acknowledge the math without feeling attacked.

### Two Paths (15 min)
Walk through Section 4.
- Path A: what's buildable on just your land (micro-hotel, tough economics).
- Path B: the assemblage play (matches the rendering, real numbers).

**Show the assemblage map.** The City/Land Bank parcels right next to his are the unlock. He may not know those are potentially available.

### The Money (15 min)
Walk through Sections 5 and 6.
- Total project cost at Path B: ~$28-30M.
- Here's where the money comes from.
- Here's what pre-development alone costs: $600K-$1.1M, 15-18 months, all at risk before construction loan funds.

**Key point:** The "funding mechanism" he's asking for on the flyer is six-figure personal risk over a year and a half. Frame it that way.

### His Equity (15 min)
Walk through Section 7.
- Three structures (sell land, contribute land as equity, contribute land as preferred equity).
- What 49% would actually require (math).
- Our recommendation: Option 2 — contribute land as equity + below-market restaurant lease + naming rights. **~10-12% equity on the hotel, 100% of Ollie's Trolley, no personal capital required.**

**How to frame:** "The 49% structure on your flyer requires you to bring 49% of the capital and 49% of the risk. What we think you actually want is: a meaningful piece of the building, your restaurant operating in it, your name on it, and cash at closing. Here's how to get all four without writing a check."

### Co-Llab's Role (10 min)
Walk through Section 8.
- We're not a developer. We're your Phase Zero partner.
- We produce the package that actually gets qualified developers to the table on fair terms.
- Fixed fee for deliverables, optional success fee.

### Close (5 min)
Don't push for a decision. Leave him with three questions (Section 9).

## Likely Objections & Responses

**"Other developers said they'd do it at 49%."**
→ "They may have said that to tie up your land. But the question is whether they can actually fund it at that structure. A signed LOI for a deal that can't close is worse than no LOI. Let's look at what gets to construction, not just what gets to a handshake."

**"I don't want to give up control of my property."**
→ "You don't have to. Options 1, 2, and 3 all preserve different kinds of control. You can sell outright for cash, contribute and stay involved as equity owner + restaurant operator, or take preferred equity with yield. Each trades some control for something else. We'll structure whichever fits you."

**"I need to own at least 50% or it's not worth it to me."**
→ "Help us understand what 50%+ represents to you — is it control, upside, legacy, something else? Because 50% of a project that can never get built is 0%. 10% of a built project is $2-3M of real value. Let's solve for the outcome, not the percentage."

**"Can't we just build what I have on the 6,000 sq ft?"**
→ "Yes, and Path A walks through exactly that scenario. It's buildable — a 24-key micro-hotel with Ollie's on the ground floor. It doesn't match the rendering, and it's harder to finance, but it's real. Some developers would prefer this path because it avoids the assemblage risk. The trade-off is smaller project, tougher economics, no major brand operator."

**"I don't want to deal with the City or the Land Bank."**
→ "Understood. That's why bringing in a developer with public-sector experience matters — they handle that, not you. If you want to stay on your 6,000 sq ft, we go with Path A. If you want the rendering's scale, someone has to navigate the City conveyance, and that's a developer's job, not yours."

**"What's your fee?"**
→ "For Phase Zero — the feasibility study, zoning analysis, market study, subsidy strategy, and developer RFP package — we'd typically do this for a fixed fee in the $35K-$75K range depending on scope. We can structure a portion as success-based at construction close if that works better for you." *(Adjust based on actual pricing.)*
